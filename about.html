About Me

I’m a Product Manager and UX Designer who works at the intersection of people, technology, and decision-making. With a background in computer engineering, I stay close to technical realities while focusing on building products that serve real user needs and business goals.

I specialize in turning ambiguous problems into clear, actionable solutions. My experience spans product strategy, discovery, UX design, and delivery, allowing me to connect insights across stakeholders, designers, engineers, and data—and turn them into products teams can confidently ship.

My Story

I started my career as a graphic designer, working closely with marketing and sales to design ads and campaign materials. That early exposure taught me how design influences communication, positioning, and business outcomes.

Curiosity then pushed me toward Android development, and during an internship, I attended a Google UX workshop. It was a turning point—I realized UX allowed me to combine my design background with technical thinking. I dove deeper, and UX soon became my career.

Later, when a Product Manager role opened up, my manager encouraged me to take the leap. I had doubts—I had no formal management background—but I chose to learn. I earned my MBA and PMP, built my management skills, and discovered my passion for product leadership.

Every role I’ve taken added a layer. Product management is where they all connect.
